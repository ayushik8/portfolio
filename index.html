01 — About

I'm an Electrical and Computer Engineering senior at UT Austin, graduating in May 2027 alongside a Certificate in Humanitarian Engineering. I like problems where the real signal is buried in noise — literally, in EEG traces and speech recordings, and figuratively, in systems where "close enough" isn't good enough.

I've written embedded C for flight-control software held to DO-178 standards at Bell Textron, built an EEG seizure-classification pipeline to 93% accuracy, and led a stress-detection wearable to a 93.47%-accuracy result and a Best Project award.

Outside the lab, I lead Texas InventionWorks' makerspace team, and helped design solar lighting for a refugee camp with the IFRC — because the same engineering that keeps an aircraft safe should also be able to light a room for someone who needs it.
